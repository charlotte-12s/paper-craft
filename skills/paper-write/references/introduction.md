# Introduction Writing Guide

## 4 CS Paper Intro Patterns

### Pattern A: Problem-Driven (most common)
1. Broad motivation (why this problem matters)
2. Current state of the art (what exists)
3. Key limitation (what's missing)
4. Our insight (what we discovered)
5. Our method (how we solve it)
6. Our results (what we achieve)
7. Paper organization

### Pattern B: Observation-Driven
1. Surprising observation (hook the reader)
2. Why this observation matters
3. Current approaches can't explain it
4. Our insight from the observation
5. Our method derived from the insight
6. Our results confirming the insight
7. Paper organization

### Pattern C: Unified Framework
1. Multiple approaches exist (show fragmentation)
2. Common thread among them (observation)
3. No unified understanding (gap)
4. Our unified framework (contribution)
5. What the framework enables (benefits)
6. Our results (validation)
7. Paper organization

### Pattern D: Technical Breakthrough
1. Technical barrier (what's blocking progress)
2. Why existing solutions fail
3. Our key technical innovation
4. What this enables (downstream improvements)
5. Our results (demonstration)
6. Paper organization

## Paragraph-Level Template (6-7 paragraphs)

| Paragraph | Content | Transition |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| P1 | Broad problem + motivation | "Despite progress, ..." |
| P2 | Current approaches + limitations | "However, ..." |
| P3 | Key insight / observation | "We observe that ..." |
| P4 | Method overview | "Based on this insight, we propose ..." |
| P5 | Key results | "Experiments show ..." |
| P6 | Contributions list (bulleted) | "Our main contributions are:" |
| P7 | Paper organization (optional) | "Section 2 ..." |

## Contribution List Format
1. We [identify/discover] [insight]...
2. We propose [method] that [key innovation]...
3. We demonstrate [result] on [benchmarks]...
