# NeurIPS — Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems

## Basic Info
- Full name: Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems
- CCF Level: A
- Acceptance rate: ~26% (2024: ~25.8%)
- Page limit: 9 pages + unlimited appendix
- Deadline: Typically May (author feedback October, notification September)
- Double-blind review: Yes

## Review Weights
- Innovation: 30% — Novelty important but not sole criterion
- Empirical: 25% — Breadth of evaluation valued
- Theory: 20% — Theoretical backing helpful
- Clarity: 15% — Well-written papers fare better
- Broader Impact: 10% — Required statement

## Writing Style
- Title: Can be broader, emphasize big picture
- Abstract: Position within broader ML landscape, highlight significance
- Introduction: Broad motivation first, then specific contribution. Must include broader impact perspective.
- Experiments: Diverse benchmarks required, show broad applicability, efficiency analysis valued

## Recent Trends (2024-2026)
- Foundation models and their properties
- Brain-inspired AI approaches
- AI alignment and safety
- Efficient training and inference at scale
- Multi-modal foundation models

## Reviewer Common Concerns
- "Too narrow, limited scope"
- "Only tested on one type of task"
- "Significance is unclear — who benefits?"
- "Missing broader impact discussion"

## Anti-patterns
- Incremental improvement on one benchmark
- Narrow technical contribution without broader implications
- Ignoring computational cost and environmental impact
- No discussion of limitations or societal implications

## Success Patterns
- Broad applicability with deep analysis
- Methods that connect multiple sub-fields
- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse settings
- Clear significance statement with broad impact discussion
