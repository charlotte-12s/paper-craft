# AAAI — AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence

## Basic Info
- Full name: AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence
- CCF Level: A
- Acceptance rate: ~20% (2024: ~20%)
- Page limit: 7 pages + supplementary
- Deadline: Typically August (notification November)
- Double-blind review: Yes

## Review Weights
- Innovation: 25% — Must advance AI broadly
- Empirical: 25% — Practical impact emphasized
- Theory: 20% — Formal grounding valued
- Clarity: 20% — Must be accessible to broad AI audience
- Significance: 10% — Relevance to AI as a whole

## Writing Style
- Title: Clear problem statement, practical impact visible
- Abstract: Emphasize practical significance and broad AI relevance
- Introduction: Start with practical motivation, connect to AI broadly
- Experiments: Show generalizability, not just narrow performance

## Recent Trends (2024-2026)
- LLM agents and tool use
- Neuro-symbolic AI
- AI for science
- Multi-agent systems
- Responsible AI and fairness

## Reviewer Common Concerns
- "How does this advance AI broadly?"
- "Too narrow for AAAI — better suited for [specialized conference]"
- "What's the practical impact?"
- "Limited to one application domain"

## Anti-patterns
- Overly narrow ML technique without generalization
- Pure theory without practical validation
- Application-only paper without methodological contribution
- Not connecting to broader AI research

## Success Patterns
- Cross-domain applicable method
- Practical impact with clear AI relevance
- General framework that works across multiple AI sub-fields
- Strong connection between theory and application
