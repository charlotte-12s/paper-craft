# ICLR — International Conference on Learning Representations

## Basic Info
- Full name: International Conference on Learning Representations
- CCF Level: A
- Acceptance rate: ~22% (2024: ~22%)
- Page limit: 9 pages + unlimited appendix
- Deadline: Typically September (author feedback January, notification January)
- Double-blind review: Yes (OpenReview)

## Review Weights
- Innovation: 40% — Breakthrough insights valued most
- Theory: 25% — Understanding "why" is critical
- Empirical: 20% — Strong results expected but secondary to insight
- Clarity: 15% — Must clearly explain the mechanism

## Writing Style
- Title: Memorable and descriptive, can be creative
- Abstract: Emphasize WHY the method works, not just THAT it works
- Introduction: Start with a question or puzzle, present insight as the answer
- Experiments: Must demonstrate understanding of mechanism, not just performance

## Recent Trends (2024-2026)
- Representation learning and feature geometry
- Grokking and double descent phenomena
- In-context learning mechanisms
- Scaling laws and emergent capabilities
- Mechanistic interpretability

## Reviewer Common Concerns
- "Lack of understanding of the mechanism"
- "Why does this work? The paper doesn't explain"
- "The method is a black box — how can we trust it?"
- "Missing analysis of failure cases"

## Anti-patterns
- Black-box improvement without mechanistic analysis
- Pure engineering tricks without understanding
- Claiming improvement without analyzing why
- Ablation that only shows "it helps" without explaining "why it helps"

## Success Patterns
- Mechanistic understanding + strong results
- Simple insight that explains complex phenomena
- Analysis that reveals previously unknown properties
- Theoretical framework that predicts empirical behavior
