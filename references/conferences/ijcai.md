# IJCAI — International Joint Conference on Artificial Intelligence

## Basic Info
- Full name: International Joint Conference on Artificial Intelligence
- CCF Level: A
- Acceptance rate: ~15% (2024: ~15%)
- Page limit: 7 pages + supplementary
- Deadline: Typically January (notification April)
- Double-blind review: Yes

## Review Weights
- Innovation: 25% — Balance of theory and application
- Theory: 30% — Formal treatment strongly preferred
- Empirical: 25% — Practical validation required
- Clarity: 20% — Must be accessible across AI sub-fields

## Writing Style
- Title: Formal and precise, theoretical contribution visible
- Abstract: Include both theoretical and practical contribution
- Introduction: Formal problem framing, mathematical motivation
- Experiments: Must validate theoretical claims, real-world applicability valued

## Recent Trends (2024-2026)
- Knowledge reasoning and representation
- Multi-agent systems
- Hybrid AI (neuro-symbolic)
- AI for social good
- Explainable AI

## Reviewer Common Concerns
- "Lacks formal treatment"
- "Theoretical claims are not rigorously proven"
- "Missing formal analysis of the method"
- "No theoretical justification for design choices"

## Anti-patterns
- Pure empirical without theoretical backing
- Informal arguments where formal proofs are expected
- Missing complexity analysis
- No formal problem definition

## Success Patterns
- Theory with practical validation
- Formal framework with real-world applicability
- Rigorous analysis with comprehensive experiments
- Clear formal problem definition and solution
